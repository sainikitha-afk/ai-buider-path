AI-Driven Prediction of Mechanical Properties in A356 Aluminum Alloys

Mechanical properties such as Yield Strength (YS), Ultimate Tensile Strength (UTS), Hardness, and Elongation are influenced by composition, microstructure, and physical mechanisms.

Traditional experimental methods are expensive and time-consuming, requiring extensive testing such as tensile and hardness tests.

The core challenge lies in nonlinear relationships between:
- Composition (Si, Mg, Fe)
- Microstructure (particle size, distribution)
- Mechanical properties

Datasets are small (~100 samples) and noisy, making machine learning difficult.

Microstructure plays a key role, especially silicon particle morphology:
- Area fraction
- Particle count
- Average size

These influence strengthening mechanisms such as dislocation obstruction and crack initiation.

The Orowan mechanism explains strengthening:
- Smaller particle spacing increases strength
- Strength is inversely proportional to spacing

Standard ML models fail due to:
- Small data
- Noise
- Weak correlations

Physics-informed approaches are required to:
- Improve generalization
- Maintain physical consistency
- Reduce overfitting

Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) are used to incorporate domain knowledge into learning.

Validation must focus on trends across regimes rather than individual samples.
